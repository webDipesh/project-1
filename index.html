Gautam Buddha

563BCE - 483BCE

Light OF Asia

Gautam Buddha, also known as Siddhartha Gautama, is often referred to as the "Light of Asia" due to his profound influence on the continent and the spread of Buddhism. Born in Lumbini, Nepal, around the 6th century BCE, he is celebrated for his wisdom and compassion, which led him to seek enlightenment after witnessing the sufferings of humanity. His teachings, which include the Four Noble Truths and the Noble Eightfold Path, emphasize the cessation of craving as the path to liberation and enlightenment. Buddha's life and philosophy were popularized by Sir Edwin Arnold's poem "The Light of Asia," which introduced Western readers to his teachings and the significance of Buddhism in Asia.

Biographies

  • Gautam Buddha was Born around 563 BCE in Lumbini (present-day Nepal).
  • Gautam Buddha was Belonged to the Shakya clan; father was King Shuddhodana, mother Maya Devi.
  • Married Princess Yashodhara and had a son named Rahul.
  • Grew up in luxury and comfort but was shielded from suffering.
  • On outings, he encountered old age, sickness, and death, which prompted a deep search for answers about human suffering.
  • At age 29, he left his royal life (renunciation) to seek truth and the end of suffering.
  • After years of study and ascetic practices, he meditated under the Bodhi Tree at Bodh Gaya.
  • On Bodh Gaya, he attained enlightenment (Buddhahood), realizing the nature of suffering and its end.

  • His teachings focused on ending suffering (dukkha) through ethical living, meditation, and wisdom.
  • He spent about 45 years traveling and teaching across northern India and Nepal.
  • Gautama Buddha passed away (entered Mahaparinirvana) at around age 80 in Kushinagar.
